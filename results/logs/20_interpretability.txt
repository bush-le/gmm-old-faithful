MODEL INTERPRETABILITY
==================================================
Reference: ML_PIPELINE_REFERENCE.md §20

GLOBAL INTERPRETABILITY
----------------------------------------
The GMM with K=2 identifies two distinct eruption regimes:

  Component 1 (weight π=0.660):
    Physical mean: eruptions=4.26 min, waiting=81.3 min
    Standardized mean: [0.68167196 0.63868815]
    Covariance (standardized):
      [0.13840753 0.06077039]
      [0.06077039 0.23654607]

  Component 2 (weight π=0.340):
    Physical mean: eruptions=1.95 min, waiting=54.8 min
    Standardized mean: [-1.32060828 -1.23733542]
    Covariance (standardized):
      [0.05431489 0.01910832]
      [0.01910832 0.18714777]

Physical interpretation:
  Component 2: SHORT eruptions (~2.0 min) with SHORT waits (~55 min)
  Component 1: LONG eruptions (~4.3 min) with LONG waits (~81 min)

This matches the well-documented bimodal behavior of Old Faithful:
short eruptions deplete less water from the underground chamber,
requiring less time to refill → shorter wait. Long eruptions deplete
more water → longer refill → longer wait.

The positive off-diagonal covariance entries confirm that eruption
duration and waiting time are positively correlated WITHIN each
cluster, not just between clusters.

LOCAL INTERPRETABILITY
----------------------------------------
For any specific data point, the responsibilities γ(n,k) from the
E-step give the probability of belonging to each component.
Points near cluster centers have γ≈1 for one component and γ≈0
for the other. Points in the transition zone have more balanced γ,
indicating genuine ambiguity — which is captured by GMM's soft
assignment but lost in K-Means' hard assignment.

FEATURE IMPORTANCE:
Both features (eruptions, waiting) contribute meaningfully to
cluster separation. The strong positive correlation (r≈0.9)
means either feature alone provides significant discriminative
power, but using both together with full covariance captures
the elongated cluster shapes that a diagonal covariance would miss.
