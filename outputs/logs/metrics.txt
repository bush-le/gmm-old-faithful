=================================================================
ANALYSIS
=================================================================

1. WHY GMM OUTPERFORMS K-MEANS ON PROBABILISTIC CLUSTERS:
   - GMM models full covariance (elliptical clusters), while
     K-Means assumes spherical clusters (equal variance).
   - Iris clusters are elongated and correlated,
     favoring GMM's covariance modeling.
   - GMM provides soft assignments (probabilities), giving
     nuanced membership for boundary points.

2. WHY KNN IS NOT SUITABLE FOR UNSUPERVISED STRUCTURE:
   - KNN is a supervised classifier — it needs labels.
   - We generated pseudo-labels from K-Means, so KNN merely
     validates K-Means consistency, not independent structure.
   - KNN cannot discover clusters, only classify points
     given an existing labeling scheme.

3. WHY HIERARCHICAL CLUSTERING BEHAVES DIFFERENTLY:
   - Agglomerative clustering uses distance-based merging
     without distributional assumptions.
   - Average linkage may create differently shaped clusters
     than the elliptical Gaussians that generated the data.
   - No probabilistic interpretation of assignments.

4. EFFECT OF COVARIANCE MODELING IN GMM:
   - Full covariance captures the correlation between
     features within each cluster.
   - This respects the natural variations in flower dimensions.
   - Without covariance modeling, boundary points would be
     misassigned, reducing clustering quality.
